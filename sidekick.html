In secondary school, social media became extremely popular and I was hooked onto it myself.

Then, I became the victim of cyber bullying.

Problem Statement
Online bullying has enabled bullies to hide behind a nameless profile, pose as someone other than themselves and - in a single click - instantly disseminate violent, hurtful or humiliating words or images.


I created Sidekick — a virtual human bot powered by AI and empathy to help victims of cyber bullying.


Assumptions and Questions

Although I have experience being bullied online myself, I still had a number of questions regarding the topic of cyberbullying.

Insights


1. It is important to have a strong support system.

All of the interviewees who had experienced being cyber bullied mentioned how having a strong support system has helped them get through the incident.



2. Accessibility to seek help for online harassment is weak.

All of the interviewees who had experienced being cyber bullied mentioned their negative experience with cyber bullying laws and how the law has failed them.


3. Low awareness on what is socially acceptable online and off.

Witnesses of bullying and cyberbullying tend to stand by the sidelines instead of approaching to help the victim.

Conclusion of Insights

Statistically, the quantitative data from research studies isn't alarming but after interviewing 7 people, the negative effects of cyber bullying is long-lasting for both victims and bullies.

Branding


A Sidekick is a secondary character whose primary role is to aid the protagonist as they work to achieve a goal and overcome an antagonist.

The primary colour of the app is green which is the international symbol for mental health awareness. Green also means growth, hope, renewal, peace, health and new beginnings.
