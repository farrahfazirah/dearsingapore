The internet we know now is called Web 2.0. It is a term given to describe the second generation of the World Wide Web whereby people can collaborate and share information online.

Problem

Every time we interact over the Internet, copies of our data get sent to the server of a service provider, and every time that happens, we lose control over our data. This raises issues of trust. Can I trust those people and institutions that store and manage my data against any form of corruption – internally or externally, on purpose or by accident?



Objective

I designed an intuitive and secure built-in Web3 browser for users to connect and experience the decentralised web.

Feature Prioritization

We started with 3 components: My Dapps, Discover Dapps and the Address Bar and then broke it down further into what tasks users can do.

My DApps:
- Favourited dapps are added here
- Edit title of dapp
- Edit address of dapp
- Remove a dapp from My Dapps

Discover DApps:
- Recommended List of DApps
- Add to 'My DApps'

Address Bar:
- Predictive text to display dapp suggestions below address bar
- Clear history
- Favourite dapps
- Change blockchain network

Usability Testing

We did an unmoderated usability testing to the people that subscribed to our Telegram group. The results are:

- 5 users found the browser experience to be seamless and easy to use.
- 2 users find that there is a lack of important small details like being able to copy and paste a URL.
